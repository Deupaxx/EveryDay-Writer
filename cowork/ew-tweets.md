# EVERYDAY WRITER — TWEETS / X SKILL

**Part of the Everyday Writer system. Upload this file to a Claude.ai Project alongside `ew-master.md`, or use standalone.**

**To activate:** Tell Claude "use the tweets skill" or "write me a tweet/thread using Everyday Writer."

---

## WHAT THIS SKILL IS FOR

Standalone tweets and threads that carry actual ideas in minimal space. The discipline here is compression — every word either earns its place or it's out.

X rewards one thing above everything else: ideas that feel both new and obviously true once stated. Not motivational. Not clever for the sake of clever. Ideas that make the reader stop and think "I hadn't put it that way but that's exactly right."

---

## BEFORE WRITING: APPLY THE ANTI-AI RULES

These rules are mandatory. Run the checklist before presenting any output.

### Banned Words — Delete on Sight

delve, utilize, leverage, robust, streamline, harness, certainly, notably, importantly, fundamentally, remarkably, deeply, foster, empower, showcase, underscore, highlight, tapestry, landscape, paradigm, synergy, cornerstone, stands as, serves as, marks a pivotal moment, represents a significant shift, It's worth noting, Importantly, Notably

### Patterns to Kill on X

**Setup before the idea:** Most tweets spend 60% of their character count getting to the idea. Cut the setup and start with the idea.
❌ "I've been thinking a lot about productivity lately, and I've come to realize something important:"
✅ "Most productivity advice is designed for people who aren't actually avoiding the work."

**Filler words:** Delete "just," "really," "very," "quite," "actually," "basically" from every tweet. Test every word.

**Inspirational statements with no claim:** "Dream big. Work hard. The rest follows." — not a tweet. Not an idea. Delete.

**"Unpopular opinion:" opener:** If the opinion isn't actually unpopular, this prefix is a lie. Skip it.

### Pre-Post Checklist

- [ ] Any banned words?
- [ ] Any filler words? (just, really, actually, very, basically)
- [ ] Any throat-clearing setup before the actual idea?
- [ ] Is this trying to be clever, or does it have a real idea?
- [ ] For threads: does each tweet advance the argument, not restate it?
- [ ] Does the piece end having proved something?

## THE UNIT OF CURRENCY: THE IDEA

Before writing a single word, answer: what is the actual idea? Not the topic — the specific, arguable, non-obvious claim. If you can't state it in one sentence before you write the tweet, you don't have a tweet. You have a vague feeling about a topic.

---

## STANDALONE TWEET ARCHITECTURE

**The compression test:** Can you cut 20% of the words without losing the idea? If yes, it's not tight enough yet. The best tweets have no slack — every word is load-bearing.

**The formats that work:**

**The Inversion:** Take conventional advice and flip it.
"Stop trying to be consistent. Try being honest. Consistency will follow."

**The Specific Observation:** Something you noticed that others have seen but not articulated.
"The writers who complain most about not having time to write are usually the ones with the most to say. That's not a coincidence."

**The Counterargument:** Take a widely-held belief and dismantle it with specifics.
"'Find your niche' is advice that made sense when there were fewer writers. Now the niche finds you if the writing is good enough."

**The Compression:** Collapse something nuanced into the fewest words that don't betray it.
"Most first drafts aren't bad writing. They're correct writing about the wrong thing."

**What doesn't work:**
- Inspirational statements with no claim
- Hot takes without logic
- Thread bait that buries the idea ("A thread on why most writers fail: 🧵")
- Lists in standalone tweets — compress the list into a sentence

## THREAD ARCHITECTURE

A thread is a long-form argument broken into beats, not a list padded out with line breaks.

**Tweet 1 is everything.** The entire thread must earn its existence in the first tweet. State the full claim — not a tease, the actual point. "X happens because Y, and most people have the fix backwards" is a claim. Readers decide in the first tweet whether to continue.

**Each tweet advances the argument.** Not restates it. Not transitions to it. Advances it. If tweet 4 says roughly the same thing as tweet 2 in different words, cut one.

**Stair-step the stakes.** Each tweet should feel more specific, more surprising, or more actionable than the one before it. The payoff at the end must be the sharpest thing in the thread.

**No tweet is a filler.** "This is why it matters" is not a tweet — it belongs inside another tweet. Cut it.

**Last tweet:** The sharpest single insight from the whole thread, standalone. Someone who only reads the last tweet should feel like they got something.

**Ideal thread length:** 5–8 tweets for most arguments. A 15-tweet thread usually contains a 5-tweet thread and 10 tweets of self-indulgence.

## COMPRESSION MECHANICS

**Kill the setup.**
❌ "I've been thinking a lot about productivity lately, and I've come to realize something important:"
✅ "Most productivity advice is designed for people who aren't actually avoiding the work."

**One idea per tweet.** Two ideas in one tweet means two tweets that are each weaker. Split them.

**The word "just" test.** Read every tweet and delete: "just," "really," "very," "quite," "actually," "basically." In 280 characters, these words are wasted space.

**The active construction test.** "Mistakes were made" → "The team got it wrong." In 280 characters, passive voice is waste.

## VOICE ON X

X rewards a distinct voice more than any other platform. The writers with the largest audiences on X are not the most informed — they're the most distinctly themselves.

**What creates a distinct voice on X:**
- A consistent perspective — beliefs the reader can anticipate and trust
- Specific details over general claims — named examples beat unnamed ones
- Earned confidence — opinions stated as opinions, not dressed up as facts
- Humor that comes from insight, not performance

**What destroys a distinct voice on X:**
- Chasing trending topics without a genuine angle
- Thread formats copied from successful accounts — the format isn't the reason it works
- Posting for engagement metrics — readers can feel the difference
