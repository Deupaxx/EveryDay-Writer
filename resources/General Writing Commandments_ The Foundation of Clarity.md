## **I. General Writing Commandments: The Foundation of Clarity**

**Directive 1: Priority on the Active Voice**

* **Law:** The subject must perform the action. Avoid the passive voice to prevent "timid" or "soft" prose.  
* **Requirement:** Identify $Active \= Subject \+ Verb \+ Object$.  
* **Example:**  
  * *Fail:* The decision was made by the committee.  
  * *Pass:* The committee decided.

**Directive 2: Mandatory Adverb Elimination**

* **Law:** Delete adverbs that modify weak verbs. Replace with a single, precise, strong verb.  
* **Constraint:** "The road to hell is paved with adverbs."  
* **Example:**  
  * *Fail:* He ran very quickly to the door.  
  * *Pass:* He sprinted to the door.

**Directive 3: The Vocabulary of Honesty**

* **Law:** Use the first word that comes to mind if it is appropriate and colorful. Never "dress up" vocabulary to sound intelligent.  
* **Constraint:** Pretentious word use is "dressing a pet in evening clothes."  
* **Example:**  
  * *Fail:* The facilitate of the remuneration was optimized.  
  * *Pass:* The payment was sent.

**Directive 4: Optimization through Habitual Input**

* **Law:** Creativity is a result of discipline, not inspiration. Establish a sacrosanct schedule.  
* **Requirement:** Morning writing must occur immediately upon system "wake-up" (30–60 minutes) before external data intake (reading/talking).

## **II. Creative and Story-Driven Commandments: The Narrative Logic**

**Directive 5: Shitty First Draft (SFD) Protocol**

* **Law:** The initial output must be uncritical and voluminous. Suppress the internal critic (Radio Station KFKD).  
* **Requirement:** Permission to be "goofy and out of control" to allow the story to "Polaroid" into focus later.  
* **Example:** Generate 2,000 words of raw narrative without backspace or revision.

**Directive 6: R.U.E. (Resist the Urge to Explain)**

* **Law:** Never explain an emotion or action that has already been demonstrated. Trust the reader's intelligence.  
* **Constraint:** If $1 \+ 1 \= 2$ is shown, stating the sum makes it $1/2$.  
* **Example:**  
  * *Fail:* "I hate you," he said angrily.  
  * *Pass:* He slammed his fist onto the table. "I hate you."

**Directive 7: Show, Don’t Tell (Sensory Immersion)**

* **Law:** Convert narrative summaries into immediate scenes using specific details and "telling details."  
* **Requirement:** Use specific nouns (mansion vs. house) and sensory associations.  
* **Example:**  
  * *Fail:* The room was messy.  
  * *Pass:* A half-eaten pizza sat on the radiator, its crust growing a coat of green fuzz.

**Directive 8: Character-Centric Plot Generation**

* **Law:** Plot must emerge from character needs, not external mandates. Do not use outlines; use "situations."  
* **Requirement:** Characters must be "flawed and complex" to maintain reader engagement.

**Directive 9: Revision Mathematics (King’s Formula)**

* **Law:** The second draft must be 10% shorter than the first.  
* **Formula:** $Draft\_{2} \= Draft\_{1} \- 0.10(Draft\_{1})$.  
* **Requirement:** Cool the manuscript in a "drawer" (buffer memory) for 6 weeks before executing the second draft.

## **III. Sales and Copywriting Commandments: The Science of Persuasion**

**Directive 10: Advertising as Salesmanship-in-Print**

* **Law:** The sole purpose of advertising is to make a measurable sale. It is not an art form or entertainment.  
* **Requirement:** Use the "salesman's standards." If a salesman wouldn't say it face-to-face, the ad shouldn't print it.

**Directive 11: The Supremacy of Specificity**

* **Law:** Platitudes and generalities ("best quality," "lowest prices") are ignored. Specific facts and figures carry inherent proof.  
* **Example:**  
  * *Fail:* Our soap makes your skin soft.  
  * *Pass:* This soap contains 25% olive oil to moisturize skin for 24 hours.

**Directive 12: The Slippery Slide Effect (Sugarman’s Axiom)**

* **Law:** The sole purpose of the first sentence is to get the reader to read the second sentence.  
* **Requirement:** Maintain momentum until the reader has read the entire body of copy.  
* **Technique:** Use "Seeds of Curiosity" (e.g., "But there's more," "Let me explain") to bridge paragraphs.

**Directive 13: Headline Efficacy and Benefit Promise**

* **Law:** 80% of the audience reads only the headline. It must contain the brand name and a specific benefit or news item.  
* **Requirement:** Promise a "large benefit" (Samuel Johnson rule).  
* **Example:** "How to Win Friends and Influence People" vs. "Social Skills Guide."

**Directive 14: Psychological Trigger Integration**

* **Law:** Use triggers to bypass rational resistance. Priority triggers include:  
  * **Involvement/Ownership:** Make them imagine using the product.  
  * **Honesty:** Admit a flaw to gain credibility for the rest of the claims.  
  * **Specificity:** Use exact numbers ($72,000$ nerve endings) to increase believability.  
  * **Exclusivity:** Flag specific audiences (e.g., "Women over thirty-five").

**Directive 15: The Persona of Communication**

* **Law:** Write in the second person singular. Pretend you are writing a personal letter to one human being.  
* **Constraint:** Use everyday conversational language. Avoid analogies and complex rhetoric.
